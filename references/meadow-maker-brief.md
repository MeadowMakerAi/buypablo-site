# Meadow Maker — Brand & Product Reference

## Competitive Thesis

Heirloom found the mass-market center of gravity in NY cannabis: approachable, effect-focused, vertically integrated, strong pricing, ~$1M/month in beverages alone. Their branding is competent but anodyne — "something you'd find on the shelf at Target." They're winning on distribution, pricing, and being first-to-scale in a supply-constrained market, not on brand loyalty. Their customers are available.

Meadow Maker's play: occupy Heirloom's territory with better design taste and tighter pricing. A brand that reads as authentic and tasteful to the cognoscenti but is priced and formatted for the mainstream buyer. The cannasseur/hash snob community drives credibility signals (budtender recommendations, shelf perception) even though they don't drive volume. Meadow Maker bridges both.

The "better but cheaper" tension is real. Without vertical integration, margin is thin. The answer: "better" lives in design and brand feel, not in out-speccing on hardware or formulation.

PABLO is trap, male, young, potency, value. Meadow Maker is the broad play — gender-neutral, mass appeal, quality-forward but accessible.

## Brand Architecture — Three Tiers, One Design Language

All tiers share the same wordmark, typographic system, and structural packaging format (image panel + type panel). Tier communicated through substrate color, photography/art style, and product descriptor copy — never through different logos or fonts.

**Tier 1 — Meadow Maker (Core):** Live resin infused prerolls, solventless vapes, standard gummies. $18–28/unit. Natural kraft paperboard. Medium format film photography of recognizable NYC locations. Launches first.

**Tier 2 — Meadow Maker Reserve:** Single-source solventless, hash holes, limited strain drops. $35–80/unit. Darker substrate (slate or charcoal paperboard). More expressive/illustrative artwork replaces photography panel. Small runs alongside or shortly after core launch.

**Tier 3 — Meadow Maker Functionals:** CBD:THC ratios, minor cannabinoid blends, low-dose gummies, beverages, adaptogen blends. $10–24/unit. White or light cream paperboard. Cleaner, more pharmaceutical-adjacent design. Post-launch phase (Q3 2026 earliest).

## Design System

**Substrate:** Heavyweight uncoated bone/off-white paperboard (~400 g/m²). The material itself communicates quality — tactile, thick, intentional. The paper is the brand.

**Wordmark:** Spaced sans-serif logotype "MEADOW MAKER." Gold foil stamp or deboss on kraft. Clean, confident, modern without being trendy.

**Typography:** Contemporary serif for any supporting type. Charcoal (#2C2C2C / PMS Black 7 C) on light substrates.

**Color palette (intentionally restrained — photography provides the color):**

| Swatch | Hex | Pantone | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kraft Natural | #C4A882 | PMS 7502 C | Primary substrate (actual kraft, not printed) |
| Warm White | #F5F0E8 | PMS 7527 C | Image panel border, Functionals substrate |
| Charcoal | #2C2C2C | PMS Black 7 C | Typography on light substrates |
| Cream Type | #E8DFD0 | — | Typography on kraft (print alternative to foil) |
| Gold Foil | metallic | PMS 10125 C | Wordmark on kraft |
| Slate | #3A3A3A | PMS 446 C | Reserve tier substrate (TBD) |

**Critical rule:** No additional brand colors. The photography IS the color. SKU differentiation via substrate shifts (kraft → slate → white) or subtle type color, never colored panels, badges, or graphic elements.

**Photography system:** Original medium format film photographs (Mamiya 7, 6×7 format). High-res TIFF scans, minimum 300 DPI. Warm film tones — do not over-correct. Core line subjects: recognizable NYC locations. Panoramic crop (~2.5:1) for packaging panel. White border framing. This creates an aesthetic that is immediately recognizable, impossible to replicate with stock, and deeply authored.

**Design principles:**
- Restraint over complexity
- Material truth over decoration
- "Medicine cabinet test" — belongs next to Aesop
- NOT wellness clichés, NOT cannabis-industry-standard leaf imagery
- "This looks intentional. This looks thoughtful."
- The devil is in the print details: typography, Pantone precision, card stock weight, finish (soft matte vs uncoated)

## Earlier Design Explorations (For Context)

Two distinct directions were explored before the current system:
1. Botanical/forest green versions (deep greens, botanical illustrations, parchment backgrounds, Playfair Display serif)
2. Cobalt blue studio version (cobalt #1B5FAE, burnt orange #D96B3A, parchment #F5EDD6, architecture-studio-meets-fragrance-house)

The current kraft/photography/restrained-palette direction supersedes both. However, elements from the cobalt direction (the confidence, the non-cannabis visual language) informed the current approach.
