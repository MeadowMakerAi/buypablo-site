## PABLO's Origin — Hemp Brand First

PABLO started as a hemp-derived CBD brand (Pablo Hemp), not a cannabis brand. Alex manufactured hemp products under cGMP standards before pivoting to NY recreational cannabis. The NY cannabis play is an expansion of an existing brand and manufacturing capability, not a cold start.

Key implication: PABLO has existing packaging files, brand assets, and print-ready artwork from the hemp era. The NY cannabis packaging is an adaptation of existing assets, not a from-scratch design project. Some of these legacy files may still be in the pipeline or informing current packaging.

## Fresh Frozen Sourcing Strategy

The live resin supply chain for PABLO:
1. "Whole plant fresh frozen CBD live resin extract" manufactured from hemp in Oregon by a partner
2. Compliant from a delta-9 perspective (<0.3% THC), legal as hemp
3. Must be imported into NY rec cannabis market through a NY-licensed hemp processor
4. Oregon → NY hemp license holder → CCC (rec license) → product becomes adult-use cannabis tracked in METRC

The Oregon partner relationship is real but Alex obscures provenance publicly — people are allowed to think PABLO makes it, which protects strategy and builds brand credibility. This is a deliberate positioning choice, not accidental.

A Brooklyn-based contact with a hemp license was identified as a potential pass-through partner. Status of that relationship is TBD. Getting their own NY hemp processing license was also discussed but timeline was unknown.
